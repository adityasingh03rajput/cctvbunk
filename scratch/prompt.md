When a student taps the LetsBunk icon, the app opens with a clean splash screen showing the glowing “LETSBUNK” logo and tagline. After a short fade, the login screen appears with two options: Login as Student and Login as Teacher. The student types in their ID and password, taps Login as Student, and the app slides into the Student Dashboard.

Here, the top shows their profile photo, name, and a small battery indicator. If the battery is low, a banner appears: “⚠ Low Battery Mode: Only attendance features active.” In the middle, a card displays today’s current lecture — subject, room, teacher, and time. Just below, a large circular attendance timer sits idle with a button labeled Start Attendance. The button is greyed out unless the phone is connected to the correct classroom Wi-Fi BSSID. Once connected, the button lights up, and on pressing it, the timer starts spinning, counting minutes in real time.

As the student keeps the app open (or minimized in recents), the timer continues. If they try to swipe it away, a warning flashes: “Don’t close — attendance timer running!”. Suddenly, mid-class, a Random Ring popup appears with a vibration: “You’ve been selected 🎲 Verify presence now.” Three choices are shown: fingerprint verification, facial scan, or a third-pass request to the teacher. A countdown clock ticks down from 10 minutes. If the student passes, their timer continues; if they fail, the app warns that this lecture may be marked absent.

Outside class time, the student can check the Calendar screen. Each date has a color — green for fully present, yellow for partial, red for absent. Double-tapping a date shows a popup: “You attended 3 lectures, 140 minutes total.” In the Profile section, they see their personal details and badges earned (like CR, Sports Club). They can also access Chat and Communities, sending friend requests, messaging classmates in a modern Instagram-style chat, or joining group communities. Important messages come through the Notices tab, where HOD or teacher announcements are listed and pushed as notifications.

The student app is simple: login → dashboard → timer → verification → history → chat/notice. It feels alive, strict on attendance, but still friendly for social and academic use.

👨‍🏫 Teacher App Tour

The teacher opens the same LetsBunk app. The splash screen is identical, but this time on the login page, they enter their ID and tap Login as Teacher. Instantly, the app opens into the Teacher Dashboard.

Instead of a timer, the teacher sees a list of their assigned classes. For example, it might show “CSE 2nd Sem – Room A102 (9:40 – 10:40)”. Tapping the class reveals an auto-updating student list — names and DPs of everyone in that class whose timers are running. If it’s a different slot, they can switch using the smart class filter, but only for classes assigned to them.

At the top of the dashboard sits a bold button: Random Ring. When pressed, the teacher selects how many students to check, and instantly, both sides respond — the teacher’s screen lists those students, while their phones buzz with the verification popup. On the teacher’s side, a neat panel shows each student’s photo, name, and verification status. Next to each name are two buttons: Present ✅ or Absent ❌. If someone doesn’t respond in time, the teacher can mark them absent. If a student had a genuine tech issue, the teacher can override and mark them present.

Another powerful option is the Whole-Day Present button — if a student forgot their phone or had an emergency, the teacher can mark them present for the full day, making their name appear in priority for all other teachers later. Teachers can also undo this if the student is later found absent.

From the Calendar screen, the teacher sees daily summaries: how many students attended, and a quick notes field to write what was taught that day. Double-tapping a student’s name anywhere shows their full profile: attendance logs, penalties, verification history, and badges assigned. Teachers also receive Notices from HOD or admin, which pop up directly in their dashboard.

The teacher app feels like a control hub: login → assigned class → live student list → random ring → attendance decisions → summaries. Strict, but efficient, with power to handle exceptions.

🖥 Admin Panel (Desktop exe) Tour

On the admin’s computer, they double-click the LetsBunk Admin Panel exe. A sleek splash screen with the LetsBunk logo flashes, then fades into a login page for admin credentials.

Once logged in, the admin sees the Dashboard — a central control center showing today’s attendance stats, upcoming lectures, and overall college attendance percentage. From here, the admin navigates through tabs on the sidebar.

First, the Room & Wi-Fi Setup tab. This is where each classroom (like A102, A201) is mapped to its Wi-Fi BSSID. The admin can test connectivity and verify that each room has a stable authorized network.

Next, the Timetable Builder. Here, the admin creates or edits lectures — entering subject name, semester, branch, class, section, teacher, and room. Attaching the classroom automatically links its BSSID, making sure student timers only work inside that class.

In the User Management tab, the admin can add new students and teachers, assign IDs, and link devices (to enforce the single-device login rule). The Biometric Upload section lets the admin connect a fingerprint scanner or webcam to capture facial data, which is then stored securely for Random Ring verification.

The Attendance Rules tab defines thresholds — for example, “50 minutes required in a 60-minute slot” with a 10-minute leverage. Penalty rules are set here too, such as appeal windows and automatic finalization after the next day’s 9:40 am.

For insights, the Reports section gives charts and exports — per-student, per-class, or entire-college attendance data, which can be saved as CSV, PDF, or Excel. The Notice Board tab lets the admin broadcast urgent messages — like exam updates or timetable changes — to all student and teacher apps instantly.

In Backup & Settings, the admin configures the database backups, restores if needed, and manages the server URL (defaulting to https://letsbunk-uw7g.onrender.com). Roles and permissions can also be fine-tuned — giving HODs partial control or letting certain teachers edit timetables.

The admin panel is the hidden backbone: open → dashboard → manage rooms, timetables, users, biometrics, rules → push notices → generate reports. It ensures everything the mobile apps rely on runs smoothly and securely."
Now, extending this robust backbone, we have built the Attendance Showcase — an incredibly powerful, native analytics module nestled right inside the Admin Panel. When the admin clicks on the "Attendance Showcase" from the side navigation, they are greeted with a beautifully designed, highly interactive dashboard. This showcase is divided into three smart views: Student View, Subject View, and Teacher View. 

In the Student View, the admin can search for any student and instantly see their complete attendance history laid out on a stunning, color-coded calendar. Days they were fully present glow in green, while absences are marked in red. The admin can click on any specific date to pull up a detailed modal showing exactly which lectures the student attended, what time they checked in, what subject was taught, and what verification methods (Wi-Fi, Face, or both) secured their attendance.

The Subject View flips the perspective. Admin selects a branch, semester, and a specific subject. They are then presented with a comprehensive calendar mapping out every single day that subject was taught. Clicking on a date reveals a deep-dive modal listing every student in that class, their individual attendance status for that lecture, and the overall class presence percentage. This is not just raw data; it’s an interactive, visually rich experience that makes tracking massive amounts of student data feel effortless.

Then comes the Teacher View, completely rewritten to harness dedicated, high-speed backend endpoints. When an admin selects a teacher, the system instantly fetches their entire assigned class allocation directly from the timetable. Beautiful, glassmorphic cards appear for each class the teacher is handling, displaying the total number of lectures conducted and the overall attendance percentage for that class. Clicking "View Details" drills down into a timeline of every lecture that teacher took for that batch. From there, clicking on a specific lecture row dynamically transforms the modal to show the exact list of students present and absent on that day, complete with a seamless "← Back to Lectures" navigation. It’s a flawless drill-down flow that provides total transparency into faculty performance and student engagement.

But the ambition of LetsBunk doesn't stop at just being a standalone product. We understand that universities and large educational institutions already have massive, legacy ERP systems in place. Asking them to completely rip out their old systems and replace them overnight is a massive friction point for adoption. To solve this, we are introducing the "Easy Integration" suite. 

In the future, LetsBunk will provide a robust set of RESTful APIs, mobile SDKs, and Server-Driven UI/UX (SDUI) components. This means universities won't need to replace their systems totally. If a college already has their own student app, they can simply plug in the LetsBunk SDK, and the Wi-Fi BSSID timer, facial verification, and Random Ring features will seamlessly embed into their existing application. For the backend, our APIs will allow two-way syncs: pulling timetable and student data from their legacy SQL databases, and pushing finalized attendance records back into their central ERP. 

With Server-Driven UI, the layout, buttons, and logic of the attendance modules can be updated directly from our servers without requiring the university to push app updates to their students. This "plug-and-play" architecture transforms LetsBunk from just a standalone product into a scalable infrastructure layer. By acting as an invisible, high-security attendance engine that powers their existing tech stack, LetsBunk eliminates adoption hurdles, drastically reduces integration costs, and positions itself as the undisputed, future-proof standard for attendance management globally.
